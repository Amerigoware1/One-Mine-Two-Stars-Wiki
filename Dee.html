Overview

Dee Cruiz is a botanist, wife of Dr. Alex Cruiz, and cherished "Aunt Dee" to the Carisco family and much of New Eden Colony. A transgender woman with dark skin and a distinctive five o'clock shadow, Dee is known for her warm, nurturing presence, vanilla-scented hugs, and exceptional botanical skills. Her journey includes carrying deep guilt over failing to protect Celectra from Dr. Kret's second experiment, and finding redemption through Celectra's profound forgiveness and love.

The Second Incident: Guilt and Trauma

During Book 1 (2232), when Amerigo was called away to Utopis-Lab 2, Celectra was left in the care of Alex and Dee. Dr. Kret arrived with falsified paperwork and bureaucratic authority:

What Happened

  • Kret presented official-looking documents authorizing him to take Celectra for "follow-up examinations"
  • Dee, despite misgivings, allowed him access, trusting the system and his authority
  • She witnessed Celectra's fear when Kret produced a sedative
  • Called Alex immediately when she realized something was wrong
  • But the delay allowed Kret to begin his isolation experiments on Celectra

The Aftermath: Years of Guilt

  • Dee carried profound guilt for years, believing she had failed Celectra
  • Her self-blame manifested as: "I should have fought harder... slammed the door... called security... hidden you"
  • She saw herself as "useless" in the face of Kret's manipulation
  • The memory of Celectra's scared face haunted her
  • She avoided discussing it but the guilt affected her relationship with Celectra

The Apology and Forgiveness

When Alex was promoted and they relocated to New Eden, Dee finally confronted her guilt:

  • She broke down in tears upon seeing Celectra, dropping to her knees to apologize
  • Celectra responded with immediate compassion and wisdom beyond her years
  • The child's forgiveness was unconditional: "You didn't fail me... You couldn't have known"
  • Celectra's profound insight: "You're not a fighter. You're a gardener. You grow things and make them beautiful."
  • Dee's redemption came through accepting that she couldn't blame herself "for not being something you're not"

"I should have fought harder. When Dr. Kret came with that paperwork, I should have slammed the door in his face. I should have called security, hidden you, or something. But I just... I let him in. I let him take you." ‚Äî Dee's apology to Celectra

Botanical Work

Utopis Station

  • Managed the station's hydroponic gardens for food production
  • Developed strains optimized for artificial light and recycled nutrients
  • Created the colony's first successful sourdough starter (a prized colony treasure)
  • Experimented with decorative plants for psychological well-being

New Eden Colony

  • Transferred her work to Ares' surface after Alex's promotion
  • Adapted Earth plants to Ares' lower gravity and different light spectrum
  • Pioneered aeroponics systems for water conservation
  • Developed the "berry jam" that becomes Celectra's favorite
  • Works with Galinstanians to understand native mineral uptake by plants

Signature Creations

  • Vanilla extract: From engineered orchids, used in cooking and personal scent
  • Berry jam: Made from hybrid berries she developed
  • Sourdough starter: Survived the journey from Earth, used colony-wide
  • Hydroponic lettuce strains: Optimized for specific light frequencies

Relationship with Celectra

Dee's bond with Celectra evolves through several stages:

Early Care (Book 1)

  • Immediate warmth and nurturing when Celectra stays with them
  • Motherly concern: asking if she's hungry, needs anything, wants to call Amerigo
  • Storytelling to comfort and connect
  • Creating a safe, vanilla-scented environment

Guilt and Distance

  • Withdrawal after the second incident, though unspoken
  • Overcompensating with food and gifts
  • Unable to meet Celectra's eyes at times
  • The unacknowledged elephant in their relationship

Healing and Redemption

  • Dee's tearful apology and knee-dropping humility
  • Celectra's four-arm hug and immediate forgiveness
  • The child's wisdom: "You grow things and make them beautiful"
  • Restored relationship, deeper than before
  • Dee becomes Celectra's confidante during pregnancy in Book 3

Celectra's summary of their relationship: "You teach me about plants and tell funny stories and make the best berry jam. That's who you are."

Personality Traits

  • Nurturing: Instinctively cares for others, especially children
  • Creative: Approaches botany as both science and art
  • Storyteller: Uses narrative to connect and teach
  • Guilt-prone: Carries responsibility heavily
  • Humble: Quick to acknowledge mistakes
  • Resilient: Has navigated transition, space travel, and personal trauma
  • Observant: Notices subtle changes in plants and people
  • Forgiving: Of others, though initially not of herself

Role in the Colony

Beyond her official botanical work, Dee serves several crucial community roles:

  • "Colony Aunt": Provides emotional support to many, not just the Cariscos
  • Food security expert: Ensures sustainable food production
  • Cultural keeper: Preserves Earth culinary traditions through her cooking
  • Psychological support: Her gardens and presence provide mental health benefits
  • Bridge builder: Helps humans understand Galinstanian "gardening" of minerals
  • Mentor: Trains younger botanists and hydroponic technicians

Legacy

Dee Cruiz represents several important themes in the colony:

  • The power of forgiveness: Both giving and receiving it
  • Alternative strength: That nurturing and growing are as valuable as fighting
  • Trans resilience: Thriving as her authentic self across two planets
  • Redemption through love: Finding self-forgiveness through others' compassion
  • Community as family: Creating chosen kinship networks
  • Healing through nature: The therapeutic power of botanical work

Her journey from guilt to redemption, facilitated by a child's profound wisdom, represents one of the most emotionally resonant arcs in the colony's history. Dee teaches that sometimes the strongest thing one can do is admit vulnerability, accept forgiveness, and continue growing‚Äîboth plants and relationships.
