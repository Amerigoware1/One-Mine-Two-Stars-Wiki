Overview

Dr. Lorain Troy served as the Chief Scientist of New Eden Colony for 35 years, providing steady leadership through the colony's most challenging periods of adaptation and first contact. A brilliant xenobiologist with deep ethical convictions, she was instrumental in establishing the scientific framework for human-Galinstanian relations. Her retirement in 2246 marked the end of an era and paved the way for Amerigo Carisco's succession.

Professional Career

Dr. Troy arrived on Ares with the founding colony wave and quickly distinguished herself as a thoughtful, methodical scientist. Her leadership style emphasized collaboration, ethical rigor, and long-term sustainability over rapid expansion.

She oversaw numerous critical initiatives including:

  • Establishing the first xenobiology protocols for Ares
  • Developing non-invasive study methods for indigenous life
  • Chairing the committee that reviewed Celectra Carisco's Ph.D. defense
  • Advocating for the ethical use of Dr. Douglas Kret's research

Throughout her tenure, she maintained that "science doesn't have morality‚Äîscientists do," emphasizing personal ethical responsibility in research.

Role in Key Events

  • First Contact Protocols: Developed the initial scientific guidelines for interaction with Galinstanians after Celectra's discovery of Stan.
  • Kret's Tribunal: Provided expert testimony during Dr. Douglas Kret's trial for unethical experimentation.
  • Celectra's Development: Monitored Celectra's unique physiology and cognition, ensuring her wellbeing while facilitating her role as bridge.
  • Retirement & Succession: Voluntarily stepped down in 2246, recommending Amerigo Carisco as her successor in a unanimous Council vote.

Relationships

Dr. Troy maintained professional relationships with most colonists, known for her fairness and integrity.

  • Amerigo Carisco: Respected colleague and chosen successor. She recognized his balanced approach to science and ethics.
  • Celectra Carisco: Served as mentor and committee member for Celectra's Ph.D. defense. Admired her unique abilities while advocating for her autonomy.
  • Councilor Webb: Often found herself diplomatically opposing his "Ares-First" policies, advocating instead for cooperative coexistence.
  • Dr. Douglas Kret: Initially supported his research until ethical boundaries were crossed; later became his most vocal critic at tribunal.

Legacy

Dr. Troy's retirement ceremony in late October 2246 honored her 35 years of service. Her legacy includes:

Scientific Ethics Framework: Established protocols that ensured New Eden's research respected indigenous consciousness and ecosystems.

Mentorship: Trained an entire generation of scientists, including Amerigo Carisco, in responsible xenobiology.

Institutional Stability: Provided consistent leadership that allowed the colony to navigate first contact and its aftermath without descending into exploitation or conflict.

In her retirement speech, she stated: "The greatest measure of our science will not be what we discover, but how we choose to use that knowledge. We have built bridges between species; may we continue to build them between our ethics and our ambitions."
